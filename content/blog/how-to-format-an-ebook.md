## Why Ebook Formatting Matters

Readers judge books by their formatting. A poorly formatted ebook loses readers on page one — even if the content is great. Good formatting makes your content look credible and easy to read.

## The Main Ebook Formats Explained

There are three formats you need to know:

**PDF** — best for guides, workbooks, and lead magnets. Looks the same on every device.

**EPUB** — best for Kindle and Apple Books publishing. Text reflows based on screen size.

**MOBI** — older Kindle format. Most platforms now prefer EPUB.

For most bloggers and content creators, PDF is the easiest starting point.

## Step by Step: How to Format Your Ebook for Free

### Step 1 — Write in Google Docs or Word

Keep your writing simple. Use Heading 1 for chapter titles and Heading 2 for subheadings. This makes formatting much easier later.

### Step 2 — Choose a Template

Start with a clean template. Avoid fancy fonts and heavy design. Simple layouts convert better and load faster.

### Step 3 — Set Your Margins

Standard ebook margins are 1 inch on all sides. This gives enough white space to make reading comfortable.

### Step 4 — Pick Your Font

Use readable fonts only. Georgia, Lato, or Open Sans work well. Keep body text between 11-13pt.

### Step 5 — Add a Cover Page

Every ebook needs a cover. Canva has free ebook cover templates that take 10 minutes to make.

### Step 6 — Export as PDF

In Google Docs go to File → Download → PDF. That's your formatted ebook ready to share.
